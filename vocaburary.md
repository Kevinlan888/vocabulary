### disparate (adj.)
- 释义: fundamentally different or distinct in kind; not allowing for comparison
- 例句: The two cultures were so disparate that communication was difficult.
- 同义词: different, distinct, unlike
- 反义词: similar, alike

### flinch (v.)
- 释义: to make a quick, nervous movement as an instinctive reaction to fear, pain, or surprise
- 例句: He didn’t even flinch when the nurse gave him the injection.
- 同义词: wince, recoil, shrink
- 反义词: confront, face

### scoff (v.)
- 释义: to speak to someone or about something in a scornfully derisive or mocking way
- 例句: The critics scoffed at his new novel.
- 同义词: mock, ridicule, sneer
- 反义词: praise, admire

### hazard (n.)
- 释义: a danger or risk
- 例句: Wet floors are a hazard for elderly people.
- 同义词: danger, risk, peril, threat
- 反义词: safety, security

### reintroduction (n.)
- 释义: the act of putting something back into use or existence after it has not been used or has disappeared
- 例句: The reintroduction of wolves into the park helped restore the ecosystem.
- 同义词: restoration, reinstatement, return
- 反义词: removal, elimination

### breed (v./n.)
- 释义: (v.) to produce offspring, usually by animals; (n.) a particular type or variety of animal or plant
- 例句: (v.) Rabbits breed rapidly. (n.) The Labrador is a popular breed of dog.
- 同义词: (v.) reproduce, procreate; (n.) type, variety, species
- 反义词: (v.) destroy, eradicate

### recognise (v.)
- 释义: to identify someone or something from having encountered them before; to acknowledge the existence or validity of something
- 例句: I didn’t recognise her at first because she had changed so much.
- 同义词: identify, acknowledge, realize, discern
- 反义词: ignore, overlook
